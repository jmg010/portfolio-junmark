Reflection

My On-the-Job Training (OJT) started on June 30, 2025, and ended on July 25, 2025, with a total of 162 hours at Upright Creative Company in the Web Development Department. My OJT experience focused mainly on Shopify theme development training and the application of what I learned through a few assigned tasks for actual client projects.

During the first few weeks, I spent most of my time learning and familiarizing myself with Shopify theme development. The company provided a structured training course that allowed me to gain knowledge and skills in several areas. I learned how to set up a Shopify development store and connect it to local development tools, install Node.js and Shopify CLI for theme creation and deployment, and use Liquid and JSON templates to design and customize pages. I also practiced creating responsive website components such as navigation menus, blog pages, collection pages, product pages, and cart pages. In addition, I applied Tailwind CSS in my designs and learned GitHub integration for collaborative version control.

While most of my tasks were focused on self-paced learning and practice, I was also given opportunities to contribute to real client projects, which provided me with a glimpse of actual freelancing work. My assigned tasks included creating a 404 page for a client’s Shopify theme, developing a rich-text section for the same client’s theme, and implementing a variant selector for the product page. These tasks allowed me to apply the skills I gained from the training course in a real-world environment where attention to detail and adherence to client requirements were essential.

Reflecting on this experience, I realized that even though the majority of my OJT involved learning and training, it was still highly valuable. I gained hands-on experience in Shopify theme development, improved my technical skills in Liquid, JSON, HTML, CSS, and Tailwind CSS, and learned to work independently while following professional standards. This OJT not only enhanced my web development capabilities but also prepared me for freelancing and client-based projects, equipping me with both the confidence and discipline needed to pursue a career in web development.
